Types Of Poker:

   There are a variety of ways to play poker, that differ depending on how cards are added to play and how many cards each player has in their hand. Below are three of the most popular types of poker and brief explanation of their rules:

Texas Hold 'Em: The most popular and easiest to learn, Texas Hold 'Em is plaid with each player being delt two pocket cards and can use any of the five communal cards to make a hand. It lasts four rounds of betting with communal cards added each round.

Omaha: Like Texas Hold 'Em, Omaha is plaid for four rounds with five communal cards, however in Omaha all the communal cards are revealed at once. It also differs from the other versions as each player has four cards in their hand.

5-Card Draw: In 5-Card draw each player is dealt 5 cards and then is allowed to use three of the communal cards instead of the cards they were originally dealt.


Poker Hands:

Royal Flush

A royal flush is five cards in order starting with a 10 and ending in an Ace, all these cards must also be in the same suit. This is the best possible hand in poker and beats all others.

Straight Flush

A stright flush is a straight, five cards in a row, as well as a flush, five cards of the same suite. However its straight can only go only as high as 9 through King, as 10 through Ace would be a royal flush.

Straight Flush

Four Of A Kind

Four of a kind is when the player has four cards with the same value, with the four diffrent suites. The last card included in the hand is the single highest avaliable cards besides the four of a kind group.

Four Of A Kind

Full House

A full house is three of a kind of one type of card value amd then a pair of two other cards of the same value. The player with the highest three of a card value wins the full house tiebreaker.

Flush

A flush is five cards of the same suite, for example five spade cards. Cards in a flush are not in order otherwise it would be a straight flush or royal flush.

Flush

Straight

A straight is five cards in order in diffrent suites, the player with the highest card value at the end of the straight wins tiebreakers. If the cards were in the same it would be a royal or straight flush.

Straight

Three Of A Kind

Three of a kind consists of three cards of the same value with diffrent suites, as well as two other unrelated high cards. Tiebreakers are settled first by who has the highest three of a kind then individual high cards.

Three Of A Kind

Two Pair

Two pair is two diffrent pairs (two cards of the same value and diffrent suites) and one extra high card. The tiebreaker is determined whoever has the highest value of pairs.

Pair

A pair are two cards of the same value but diffrent suites. It also consists of three additional cards that have no other pairs, otherwise it would be a full house or two pair.

Pair

High Card

When there is no pairs, flushes or straights, then the hand's value is determined by its highest value card. Ace is usually considered the highest, followed by face cards than number cards.
